About

I am a PhD Candidate in Imaging Science at Washington University in St. Louis (WashU) under Prof. Aris Sotiras, focusing on interpretable machine learning for big data. I hold a Master's of Science in Data Science and Analytics from WashU's Electrical & Systems Engineering Department.

My PhD research centers on representation learning in large-scale neuroimaging, aging, and Alzheimer's disease. I develop scalable machine learning algorithms enabling the extraction of interpretable, low-dimensional representations of complex brain data, which facilitates the study of disease progression, heterogeneity, and biomarkers across large-scale, multi-modal datasets.
